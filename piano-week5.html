Core Theory

♯♯♯ A Major — Three Sharps

The Circle of Fifths Keeps Moving

C(0♯) → G(1♯) → D(2♯) → A(3♯). Three sharps: F#, C#, G# — appearing in exactly this order. Apply W-W-H-W-W-W-H from A:

1A
2B
3C#
4D
5E
6F#
7G#
8A

Key chords in A: Chord 1 = A–C#–E  ·  Chord 4 = D–F#–A (= Chord 1 in D!)  ·  Chord 5 = E–G#–B  ·  Chord 6m = F#–A–C# (F#m)

The Axis in A: A–E–F#m–D. This progression is in “Chasing Cars” (Snow Patrol), “Use Somebody” (Kings of Leon), and “Africa” (Toto).

🎼 Sus Chords — The Floating Sound

Suspended Between Tension and Rest

A sus chord replaces the 3rd with the 2nd (sus2) or 4th (sus4). Result: a chord that sounds neither major nor minor — it floats. The sus4 leans forward wanting to resolve; the sus2 is open and airy.

Cmaj = C–E–G
Csus4 = C–F–G(replace E with F)
Csus2 = C–D–G(replace E with D)

Play Csus4 → C → Csus2 → C. Hear sus4 lean forward, sus2 float open, and C major land solidly. Sus chords appear in “Here Comes the Sun,” “Every Breath You Take,” “Pinball Wizard,” and countless others.

🎜 Melody by Ear — Any Song, No Sheet Music

The Four-Step Method

Hum the first note of a melody → find it on piano → hum the second note (higher or lower?) → step and check. Start with “Happy Birthday” — a melody you know perfectly, so the question mark is only “where is it on the piano?”

1 Hum→2 Find→3 Hum next (up or down?)→4 Step & check

Every melody you find makes the next one faster. By Week 8 you will find the opening phrase of any song within 5 minutes of hearing it — with no sheet music, no app, just your ear and your hands.

Sus2 and Sus4

Csus2 = C–D–G. Csus4 = C–F–G. Play: Csus4 → C → Csus2 → C slowly. Then on Chord 5: Gsus4 → G → Gsus2 → G. Sus4 leans forward; sus2 floats open; major lands solidly.

Sus Chords
10min

Sus Decoration

Play 1–5–6m–4 in C. Before every chord change, play its sus4 for 2 beats: Csus4–C → Gsus4–G → Amsus4–Am → Fsus4–F. Each chord is announced by its suspended version — exactly how pop producers use sus chords.

Teacher's Notes

👂

Melody by Ear Is the Goal

Finding a melody you know on piano is the single most important milestone so far. It feels slow the first time. By Week 8 you’ll do it in under 5 minutes for any song.

♯♯♯

Three Sharps, No Problem

A Major has three black keys. Feels unfamiliar for 2 days, then natural. Your hands already know where F# lives from D major.

🌊

Sus = Decoration, Not Replacement

Sus chords are ornaments on major chords — always resolve them. Sus4 → major creates tension then release. That movement is what makes them beautiful.

🎵

Hum Everything

Hum every note before you play it. This creates a direct neural pathway between sound and finger position, the core of playing by ear.

⭕

The Circle Is Your Map

You know C, G, D, A — four consecutive steps clockwise on the Circle. Each step adds one sharp. Week 6 goes the other direction: F Major with one flat.

📅

Listen Deliberately

Pick one pianist you admire: Norah Jones, Ben Folds, John Legend, Billy Joel. Listen to 3 songs and try to identify: key? chord rhythm? sus chords? Deliberate listening is equal in value to practice time.
