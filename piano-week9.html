What this week is about

E Major adds four sharps (F#, C#, G#, D#). You now know every common sharp key. This week you also explore harmonic minor — the version of minor that raises the 7th degree, creating the most dramatic resolution in music. Your second complete arrangement this week uses a minor key as its tonal home. Song: “Nothing Else Matters” by Metallica — E minor, arpeggiated LH, beautiful melody. A song that sounds vastly more sophisticated than it actually is to play.

♯♯♯♯

E Major

Four sharps: F#, C#, G#, D#. The last major key you will learn in this course. E major has a bright, almost electric quality.

🌙

Harmonic Minor

Natural minor raises the 7th by one half-step: in A minor, G becomes G#. Creates an exotic, dramatic pull to the tonic. Used in Baroque music, flamenco, and film scores.

📋

Arrangement #2

Your second complete arrangement — this time in a minor key. All the same section structure but with a completely different emotional colour.

🎸

Minor Key Ear

Hearing the difference between natural minor (gentle) and harmonic minor (dramatic) by ear. The raised 7th creates a distinct tension not found in any other scale.

Build the Harmonic Minor

A harmonic minor: A–B–C–D–E–F–G#–A. Raise the 7th by one half-step. Play natural minor then harmonic minor side by side. Hear the dramatic difference on the 7th degree: G natural is gentle; G# creates an urgent pull back to A.

Harmonic Minor
10min

Harmonic Minor Chord: V7 in Minor

In A harmonic minor, the 5th chord (E) gains a major quality AND a dominant 7th: E7 = E–G#–B–D. Play Am–Dm–E7–Am. The E7 resolution back to Am is one of the most dramatic cadences in all of music.

Teacher's Notes

♯♯♯♯

Four Sharps Is Still One Formula

E major has four black keys but the W-W-H-W-W-W-H formula delivers all of them automatically. You didn’t learn four new facts — you applied one formula.

🌙

Harmonic Minor = Drama

The raised 7th (G# in A minor) creates a half-step pull to the tonic that is far more urgent than natural minor’s whole-step. This is why harmonic minor sounds so intense.

📋

Minor Arrangements Are Different

In a minor key arrangement, the emotional arc is different. The “home” chord feels tense rather than resolved. Build toward moments of release, not moments of arrival.

🎸

Nothing Else Matters Is More Accessible Than It Sounds

The arpeggiated pattern sounds very advanced but is actually four simple chords played one note at a time. Start with just the four notes of the arpeggio before adding dynamics.

🔊

30-Second Detection Drill

The 30-second key detection drill is your most powerful ear training exercise at this stage. Do it weekly. Track your score. By Week 12 you should be 9–10/10 consistently.

📅
