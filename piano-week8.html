What this week is about

This is the week your playing takes on a new dimension. Major 7th chords (Cmaj7 = C–E–G–B) shimmer with sophistication. Minor 7th chords (Am7 = A–C–E–G) have a warm, melancholic richness. Together with the dominant 7th you learned last week, you now have all three 7th chord qualities. This week’s song: “Hallelujah” by Leonard Cohen — a song that uses major 7th, minor 7th, and major chords in a perfect harmonic arc. You will build your first complete arrangement: intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro.

✨

Major 7th (Imaj7)

C–E–G–B. Dreamy, sophisticated, slightly suspended. The sound of jazz piano ballads. Instantly upgrades any intro or outro.

🌙

Minor 7th (IIm7)

Am7 = A–C–E–G. Warm, rich melancholy. Softer than a plain minor chord. Used in bossa nova, jazz, and every R&B ballad.

🔄

ii–V–I Full

Dm7–G7–Cmaj7: the complete jazz cadence. All three 7th types in one motion. The deepest harmonic grammar in tonal music.

📋

First Full Arrangement

Intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro — five distinct sections, each with a different character. This is how musicians build a complete performance of one song.

Daily Practice Plans

Monday — Day 50Major 7th
5min

Seven-Key Warmup

1–5–6m–4 in C, G, D, A, F, B♭ — no stopping.

Warmup
12min

Build Major 7th Chords

Cmaj7 = C–E–G–B (add the 7th above the root — just one half-step below the octave). Gmaj7 = G–B–D–F#. Amaj7 = A–C#–E–G#. Play each. The maj7 has a dreamy, shimmering quality — like a major chord that hasn’t quite fully landed.

Major 7th
8min

Maj7 in Context

Play Axis in C but upgrade Chord 1: Cmaj7–G–Am–F. Hear how the intro and outro feel softer and more sophisticated with the major 7th. Intro: Cmaj7 (held, 4 beats). Then into the Axis. The maj7 opening sets a mood before the progression begins.

Application
5min

Maj7 vs Dom7 vs Major

Play C major, then Cmaj7, then C7 in sequence. Three chords, three completely different emotional qualities: plain (major), dreamy (maj7), urgent (dom7). 5 repetitions to lock in the distinction.

Ear Training
Tuesday — Day 51Minor 7th

Maj7 Review

Cmaj7, Gmaj7, Amaj7, Fmaj7 — one at a time, then in sequence. The major 7th is now part of your vocabulary.

Review
12min

Build Minor 7th Chords

Am7 = A–C–E–G (add the 7th to Am). Dm7 = D–F–A–C. Em7 = E–G–B–D. Play each. The min7 is warmer and richer than plain minor — less sharp, more enveloping. Listen especially to Am7: that warm, jazzy colour is everywhere in R&B and bossa nova.

Minor 7th
10min

ii–V–I Full Jazz Motion

Dm7 → G7 → Cmaj7. This is the complete jazz cadence: minor 7th → dominant 7th → major 7th. Play it 10 times slowly. Each chord leans inevitably into the next. This three-chord motion is the grammar of jazz harmony.

ii-V-I
3min

7th Chord Ear Test

Play one of three: Am, Am7, C7 randomly. Identify by ear. The 7th adds a subtle but distinct colour to each. 10 rounds.

Thursday — Day 53Voice Leading 7ths
5min

Wrist Rolls + Octave Stretch

Roll both wrists 10 times each direction. Then stretch to octave on each hand. Never play with tension in the wrist.

Flexibility
12min

Voice Leading With 7th Chords

Cmaj7 to Am7: keep E and G in place, only move C→A and B→(stay or →G). Smooth voice leading makes 7th chord progressions sound orchestral. Practise transitions: Cmaj7→Am7→Dm7→G7→Cmaj7.

Voice Leading
8min

7th Chords in LH

Try playing full 4-note 7th chords in LH as accompaniment. Cmaj7 (low C to B) in left hand while RH plays melody. The rich LH chord supports the melody from below. Start with slow whole notes, LH only, then add RH melody on top.

5min

Find Cmaj7, Am7, Dm7, G7 eyes closed. All four in one round, 3 rounds. The 7th chord shapes have their own tactile fingerprints.

Teacher's Notes

✨

The Major 7th Is Magic

Cmaj7 instead of C on your intro or outro chord — try it once and you’ll use it forever. That shimmering B above the chord is the sound of sophistication.

🌙

Minor 7th Softens Everything

Am7 instead of Am: warmer, less sharp. Every minor chord in your arrangements can be upgraded. The result is richer, more sophisticated sound.

🔄

ii–V–I Is Everywhere

Dm7–G7–Cmaj7 appears in virtually every jazz standard ever written. Learning it now means you can understand and play jazz harmonic language immediately.

📋

Arrangement Sections Matter

Intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro each need a different character. If they all sound the same, you have one long section — not an arrangement. The transitions between sections are where the music breathes.

🎭

Layer Complexity Slowly

Start with just chords. Add LH pattern. Add melody. Add dynamics. Add 7th upgrades. Each layer added after the previous one is solid. Never try to do everything at once.
