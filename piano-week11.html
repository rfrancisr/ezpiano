What this week is about

Everything is built. This week is about refinement — taking three complete arrangements and making them sound like a musician, not a student. Sustain pedal adds warmth and resonance. Embellishments (grace notes, turns, fills, chord rolls) add personality. Phrasing arcs give each arrangement a deliberate emotional shape from intro to outro. And the four-pass polish method ensures every arrangement is performance-ready. There is no new song this week — the three you have are the goal.

🎹

Sustain Pedal

Depress just after the chord lands, release just before the chord changes. The resulting resonance transforms the piano’s sound. Syncopated pedaling is the professional technique.

✦

Embellishments

Grace notes (lean in), turns (note above–note–note below–note), passing fills (ascending run), chord rolls (arpeggio upward). One per phrase — not one per bar.

〜

Phrasing Arc

Every arrangement has a shape: intro (quiet, establish) → verse (build) → chorus (peak) → bridge (contrast) → outro (resolve). Draw it on paper and play it with intention.

📋

Four-Pass Polish

Pass 1: Structure. Pass 2: Harmony (7th upgrades in place?). Pass 3: Expression (dynamics, rubato). Pass 4: Performance (at tempo, no stopping, recorded).

Sustain Pedal Introduction

If you have a pedal: practice syncopated pedaling on C major scale first. Depress pedal just after each note, hold through, release just before the next note. Then apply to Axis in C: depress after each chord lands, release just before changing. No pedal: hold each chord’s lowest note slightly longer to simulate resonance. This “finger pedaling” teaches the same principle.

Teacher's Notes

🎹

Polish Is Not Perfection

The goal is honest and expressive playing, not flawless playing. A slightly imperfect arrangement played with genuine feeling is more musical than a technically accurate arrangement played mechanically.

✦

One Embellishment Per Phrase

Every student discovers embellishments and immediately overuses them. One in the right place is worth ten scattered. Save them for arrivals: the start of a chorus, the final note of a section.

〜

Draw the Arc

Before playing an arrangement, draw its dynamic shape on paper. Intro height, verse height, chorus peak, bridge contrast, outro resolution. Then play the shape, not just the notes.

🎤

Play for Someone

If possible: play your three arrangements for one other person this week. Even once. Playing for a listener changes how you play — it activates the performance mindset that makes Week 12 easier.

📋

Saturday Free Play Is Not Optional

Unstructured play is how your brain integrates everything it has learned. After 11 weeks of directed practice, your musical intuition needs space to operate without instructions.

✅

You Are a Pianist

There’s a moment somewhere in Week 11 when the word “beginner” stops applying. You have a harmonic vocabulary, a repertoire, and ears that hear music differently. Own it.
