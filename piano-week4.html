What this week is about

Three weeks of chords have been building the stage. Now the star walks out. This week your right hand learns to play single-note melodies while your left hand holds the harmony. D Major adds two black keys (F# and C#), the 3 minor chord gives you the introspective sound of film scores and ballads, and you’ll play your first melody over chords using the C Major Pentatonic — five notes with no wrong answers. Core Theory

🎵 D Major — Two Black Keys

The Pattern Keeps Working

Apply W-W-H-W-W-W-H from D: you hit F# at step 3 and C# at step 7. Two black keys, both already familiar from G major and the Circle of Fifths.

Key chords in D: Chord 1 = D–F#–A  ·  Chord 4 = G–B–D (same as Chord 1 in G!)  ·  Chord 5 = A–C#–E  ·  Chord 6m = B–D–F# (Bm)

The 3 minor chord in C = Em (E–G–B). In G = Bm (B–D–F#). In D = F#m (F#–A–C#). It has a quiet, introspective quality — not as dark as 6m, not as yearning as 2m. Play 1–3m–4–5 in C (C–Em–F–G): hear the gentle, reflective arc of songs like “Someone Like You.”

The Descending Minor Chain

Play 3m–6m–2m–5–1 in C: Em–Am–Dm–G–C. One of the most beautiful progressions in all of music — descends through all three minor chords then resolves home. Used in Bach, Pachelbel, Coldplay, and countless film scores.

3m→6m→2m→5→1→home
🎵 Playing Melody — The Right Hand Sings

Left Hand Holds, Right Hand Sings

Until now both hands moved together. This week the roles split: LH plays the chord progression (rhythm section), RH plays single notes as melody (the singer). Start with the C Major Pentatonic: C–D–E–G–A. Five notes with no 4 or 7 — zero wrong answers over any C major chord.

LH: 1–5–6m–4 loop+RH: pentatonic melody=First real music

Melody notes that land on chord tones (1, 3, 5 of the current chord) sound most stable. Non-chord tones create movement and tension. Leave rests — silence is as important as the notes. A rest in a melody is where it breathes.

Teacher's Notes

🎵

Melody Changes Everything

The moment RH plays a melody while LH holds chords is the moment piano starts feeling like music, not exercises. Even 3 notes will feel different. Let that feeling land.

♯♯

Black Keys Are Friends

F# and C# will feel awkward for 2–3 days. Your hand will adjust. Every new key’s black key pattern eventually feels like home.

🔇

Silence Is Melody

Most beginners fill every beat. Great melodies are half rests. Play a note, hold it, then play another. The space between notes is where melody breathes.

📱

Record Yourself

Your phone is your best teaching tool. Record 30 seconds each Sunday. You’ll hear things your hands don’t feel.

🔗

Keys Are Connected

C’s Chord 5 (G) IS G’s Chord 1. G’s Chord 5 (D) IS D’s Chord 1. Every key you learn gives you chords you already know from the adjacent key.
