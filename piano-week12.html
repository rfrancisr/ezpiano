What this week is about

Week 12 introduces nothing new. Every skill is built. Every chord is known. Every key is mapped. This week is entirely about performing what you have with confidence, intention, and musical presence. Monday–Tuesday: final preparation. Wednesday: ear demonstration (5 cold songs, scored). Thursday: dress rehearsal. Friday–Saturday: rest. Sunday: the final recording session — your three-piece recital.

🎼

The Three-Piece Set

Opening piece (most confident), centrepiece (most emotional), closing piece (strongest resolution). A deliberate order creates a complete musical experience.

📊

Ear Demonstration

5 songs you have never practiced, played cold. Start within 15 seconds, stay 2 minutes, map the full chord loop. Score out of 20. A document of how far your ears have come.

🎤

The Performance Mindset

Practicing means stopping when something goes wrong. Performing means continuing no matter what. The moment you decide to continue through a mistake, you are performing.

Record this session. 5 songs you have never specifically practiced — random from shuffle. For each: start playing within 15 seconds, stay 2 minutes, then pause and write: key, major/minor, chord loop in numbers. Score: key correct = 1 pt, major/minor = 1 pt, progression = 2 pts. Maximum 20 points. Whatever you score — it represents growth from zero to musician.

The Recital — Record Your Best Takes

Two takes of each arrangement, then one continuous three-piece recital recording. The instruction is simple: inhabit the music. Not “play the notes correctly.” Not “don’t make mistakes.” Be inside the sound, inside the harmony, inside the phrase. Whatever happens technically — if you are present in the music, the recording will sound like a musician.

What Comes Next

🎵 Six Paths Forward

The Course Ends. The Music Doesn’t.

More repertoire: Apply the arrangement blueprint to 5 more songs you love. By your 8th arrangement, the process is instinctive. Build a set of 10 pieces you can play anytime.

Jazz & Neo-Soul: You have the ii–V–I, all 7th chords, and maj7 fluency. Next: chord extensions (9ths, 11ths, 13ths) and jazz voicings. Start with Herbie Hancock or Norah Jones transcriptions.

Songwriting: You know how chord progressions create emotion. You know how melody sits over harmony. Write a song. Start with a chord loop, add a melody by ear, see what words come.

Playing with others: Find a jam session, an open mic, a musician friend. Your real-time ear and number system mean you can communicate with any musician instantly.

Deep ear training: Pursue interval recognition by name. Apps like Tenuto or EarMaster take your current ear and build it systematically. The skill that separates musicians who can transcribe anything from those who can only approximate.

Explore other styles: Gospel, Latin, classical counterpoint, blues improvisation, bossa nova. Each is a universe that builds on everything you’ve built. Pick the style whose music moves you most and go deep.
